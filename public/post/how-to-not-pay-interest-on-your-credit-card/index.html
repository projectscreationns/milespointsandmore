How to Not Pay Interest on Your Credit Card

FinanceDebt
Updated Apr 9, 2025

Are you someone who carries a balance on their credit card and pays interest every month?

Here's an idea: stop making the banks easy money. Four ways to pause or kill the interest without hurting your credit score:

Balance transferOpen a new card with a 0% intro offer and move your debt onto it
Call & negotiateAsk your bank for a lower rate. They'll do a rate review
Ask for a 0% promoBefore any big purchase, ask if you have an interest-free promotion
Check existing cardsYour current cards may already have a balance-transfer promo waiting

Step 1: Balance transfer to a new card

Look for a 12, 18 or 21-month interest-free balance transfer offer (in Australia you'll even see crazy 36 and 48-month promos).

Here's the whole idea in one picture. Say you owe $10,000 at a typical ~25% rate, and you move it to a new card with 12 months at 0% and a 3% transfer fee:

Do nothing
~$2,500
interest over the next 12 months
Balance transfer
$300
one-time 3% fee — then $0 interest for a year

That's it. One application, one fee, and the meter stops running. Your score takes a small short-term hit from the new card, and it bounces back as you keep making payments.

Step 2: Call your bank and negotiate

Easy step that hardly anyone does. Just call. If the agent isn't helpful, hang up and try again later. You're making the bank money either way — they will agree to a rate review.

Step 3: Ask for a 0% promo before a big purchase

Got a big bill coming? Call and ask if you have any 0% interest promotion — you usually get one every year if you never miss payments. Typically Discover runs 12 months interest-free, Bank of America about 9, and Barclays is showing 10 for me right now.

Step 4: Check the cards you already have

The best option of all. Look in your existing cards' promo tabs (or call and ask) for an interest-free balance transfer promo. Note: the transfer has to go to a different card, not the same one. Approval takes 1–2 weeks, then you're just making minimum payments, interest-free.

You can rinse and repeat this — but make smart choices. A dollar today is better than a dollar tomorrow. Use debt to your advantage.
