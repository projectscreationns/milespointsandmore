How to Not Pay Interest on Your Credit Card

FinanceDebt
Updated Apr 9, 2025

Are you someone who carries a balance on their credit card and pays interest every month?

Here's an idea: stop making the banks easy money. There are a couple of ways you can actually reduce or pause the interest paid every month without affecting your credit score.

Here's what you can do:

  1. Open a new credit card and do a balance transfer.
  2. Call your bank and negotiate a better interest rate.
  3. Call your bank and ask for an interest-free promotion before you make a big purchase.
  4. Look for an existing credit card that may have a balance transfer promotion.

Step 1: Open a new card and do a balance transfer

Make sure the new account has a 12-, 18- or 21-month interest-free balance transfer offer. In Australia you'll even see some crazy 36- and 48-month promos.

Do a simple calculation first (just use ChatGPT). Example: you owe $10,000 on Chase card X. Open Chase card Y with a 12-month 0% intro and a 3% balance transfer fee. You move the $10,000 from X to Y and get hit with a $300 fee — but no interest for a year.

Opening a new card means your score takes a small short-term hit. It bounces back if you keep making payments.

Step 2: Call your bank and negotiate a better rate

Easy step that hardly anyone does. Just call. If the agent isn't helpful, hang up and try again later — eventually you get someone who'll help. You're making the bank money either way, so they'll agree to a rate review.

Step 3: Ask for an interest-free promo before a big purchase

Got a big bill coming? Call and ask if you have any 0% promotion. You usually get one every year if you never miss payments. Typically Discover has a 12-month interest-free, Bank of America a 9-month, and Barclays is showing a 10-month for me right now.

Step 4: Check your existing cards for a balance transfer promo

This is the best option. Look in your existing cards' promo tabs, or call and ask. Note: balance transfers must go to a different card, not the same one. Once approved (1–2 weeks) you stop paying interest and make minimum payments for the promo period.

You can rinse and repeat, but make smart choices. A dollar today is better than a dollar tomorrow — use debt to your advantage.
