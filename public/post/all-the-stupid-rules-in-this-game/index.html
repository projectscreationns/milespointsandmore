All the Stupid Rules in This Game

RulesCredit Cards

There are way too many rules to look out for when churning for credit card points.

I lean on Chase because they offer strong rewards and let you earn bonuses again, unlike American Express. Chase and similar issuers let you reapply after 2–4 years. Amex's "one bonus per lifetime" is basically forever.

Here's what each issuer is doing:

  • Chase — 5/24: open 5+ cards in 24 months and you'll get denied.
  • Amex: one welcome bonus per card, and "lifetime" means 7+ years.
  • Citi: 48-month restriction on airline cards; 24 months for other cards.
  • Bank of America: 2 cards in 2 months, 3 in 12 months, or 4 in 24 months.
  • Capital One: max 2 personal cards at a time.
  • Wells Fargo: one bonus every 15 months per card.
  • Barclays: denies you with 6+ accounts opened in 24 months.
  • U.S. Bank: no bonus if you opened another of their cards in the past 12 months.
Learn these before you apply. One wrong order and you burn a 5/24 slot for nothing.

None of this is financial advice. Everything here is simply an opinion. Always check current terms with the provider and consult a licensed financial adviser before making any financial decisions.
