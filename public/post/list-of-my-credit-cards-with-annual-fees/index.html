List of My Credit Cards with Annual Fees

Credit CardsStrategy

I keep a handful of annual-fee cards, mostly for travel benefits — travel insurance, free night stays, purchase protection and return protection. Here's the list and why each one stays in my wallet.

1. Amex Marriott Bonvoy

Annual free night certificate for properties up to 35,000 points. The $95 fee is easily offset — redemptions often clear $200+ a night; I recently used one at a JW resort where rates hit $500. You also get 15 automatic elite nights a year. Combined with the business version that's 30 nights, and with promos I hit Platinum, which unlocks Nightly Award Upgrades for suites.

2. Chase United Card

$0 annual fee the first year, with strong travel protections: baggage delay insurance (up to $100/day for 3 days), lost luggage reimbursement (up to $3,000/passenger), trip delay reimbursement (up to $500/ticket), trip cancellation/interruption (up to $1,500/person, $6,000/trip), and primary auto rental collision coverage.

3. Amex Business Marriott Bonvoy

Complements the personal Marriott card — together that's 30 automatic nights a year, and the free night certificate adds two more toward status.

4. Chase Hyatt Card

A free night every year at Hyatt properties — even premium ones like Alila and Hyatt vacation clubs participate. The free night usually outweighs the annual fee, and I consistently get room upgrades. (Currently not accepting new applications.)

5. Amex Blue Cash Preferred

I added this one specifically for 90-day return protection. Great for buying from retailers with strict refund policies — buyer protection on the purchases you're not 100% sure about.

Every one of these earns back its fee in benefits I actually use. That's the only reason to pay an annual fee.

None of this is financial advice. Everything here is simply an opinion. Always check current terms with the provider and consult a licensed financial adviser before making any financial decisions.
