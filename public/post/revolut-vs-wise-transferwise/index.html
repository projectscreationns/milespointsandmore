Revolut vs Wise (TransferWise)

ComparisonTransfersFinance

The question still remains — which one is better? Cheaper, faster, more reliable? I've used both, so let me break down the numbers so you don't have to.

Rate and fees

Let's send $10,000 AUD to USD. Revolut shows $6,758.11 USD. Note transferring is a 2-step process with Revolut — the exchange has no fee on weekdays (1% on weekends), and moving funds to a US account costs $5.39 USD. So you end up with $6,752.72 USD.

Wise's process is more straightforward. Sending the same $10,000 AUD, you receive a total of $6,693.95 USD. Total fees charged: 48.39 AUD. Honestly I'm appalled by the difference.

Revolut is significantly cheaper and offers a better rate. Even with the weekend 1% fee, Revolut still comes out on top. I tested USA to Germany too — same result, favouring Revolut.

How do they make money?

Revolut uses the mid-market rate and profits off the spread. Wise charges a sizeable upfront fee.

Who's faster?

For the transfer above: Wise quoted 2 business days, Revolut quoted same day. Revolut, clearly.

The differences that actually matter

Wise lets you lock in a rate for 36 hours and cancel if you change your mind — good for mitigating rate risk. Revolut uses live market rate. The other difference is currency coverage: Wise supports more currencies (convert to 52, send to 70 countries) than Revolut.

For straight-up money transfers, Revolut beat Wise by enough that it's a no-brainer. Wise wins if you need rate lock-in or exotic currencies.
