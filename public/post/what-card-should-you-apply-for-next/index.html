What Card Should You Apply for Next?

StrategyCredit Cards
Updated Apr 24, 2025

I get this question all the time. So please read.

Let's assume you have a couple of cards and a credit score over 700+. Now you can look into churning.

Step 1: Make sure you're under 5/24. That means approved for under 5 cards in the past 24 months. (How to check? Go into Credit Karma and see the card open date for every card you have.)

Step 2: Decide if you want Travel or Statement Credit.

Step 3: If you decide travel, think about which airline or air alliance.

Only then do you look at the cards. Otherwise it's just overwhelming with the amount of cards available.

Some ideal* examples for travel: if you're living in Denver or Houston, think United Explorer. If you're in Dallas or Charlotte, think AA card. Ideal means it's nice to have — I've had a lot of luck with all 3 alliances.

This incorporates the best strategy at this time. It does include business credit cards. It's not hard to open an LLC just for sign-up bonuses — it's inexpensive and quick. As long as you stick to getting SUBs with miles and points and NOT cash, you should be fine on taxes.

As far as I can find: IRS Notice 2008-64 states that bonus miles or points could be considered taxable as income if provided without any associated spending requirement. So any card with a minimum spend requirement for the SUB qualifies. I could be wrong, so do your own research.
