Best High-Interest Savings Account in Australia (March 2023)

AustraliaSavingsFinance

Looking for the best savings account in Australia? With so many options it's hard to know where to start. Here's a look at three of the best right now — plus the ones worth a mention.

BOQ Savings for 18–35 year olds

BOQ is offering 5.15% p.a. for customers aged 18–35 — one of the highest rates going, with no monthly fees. To get the 5.15% you must deposit at least $1,000/month into your BOQ Everyday account and make 5 eligible transactions per month. Set up BPAY or automatic payments to hit the 5 transactions. New customers earn interest from the first month. Downside: their app is poorly built and the max balance is capped at $10,000.

ING Savings

ING is at 4.8% p.a., no monthly fees, deposit and withdraw anytime. To get the rate you deposit at least $1,000/month, make 5+ purchases on your linked ING card each month, and (new in March 2023) grow your balance excluding interest. No maximum balance limit.

Macquarie Honeymoon Saver

4.5% p.a. for the first four months, then it drops to 1.35% p.a. No monthly fees, 24/7 access. Good short-term, watch the drop-off.

Also worth a mention

  1. Rabobank — 4.75% for the first 4 months
  2. HSBC — 4.5% for the first 3 months
  3. Move Growth Saver — 4.75% up to $25,000 (deposit $200/month, no withdrawals)
  4. UBank — 4.35% (deposit $200/month)

Personally I'm not a fan of Rabobank — still doing banking in the stone age, slow to open and activate. HSBC is great but the app needs work. Move Growth Saver has given a lot of people ID-verification headaches (they don't seem to use Equifax).

I'd vouch for UBank — simplicity, and you don't have to jump through hoops.

Whatever you pick, read the terms carefully for fees, eligibility and requirements.
